I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Nuclear and Quantum Engineering at KAIST, working on physics-aware and data-driven medical imaging methods for CT image quality improvement. Research

Research Interests

My work develops robust reconstruction and enhancement methods for medical and industrial X-ray imaging, combining CT physics, iterative reconstruction, and neural networks.

AI-based CT image reconstruction Low-dose & sparse-view CT imaging CT artifact reduction Metal, beam hardening, scatter, and motion artifacts CT geometry calibration Diffuse optical tomography Industrial non-destructive imaging

Dissertation

Development of neural network-based image reconstruction methods for CT image artifact reduction and dose reduction

Developed deep learning-based image reconstruction methods for improving CT image quality under artifact-corrupted and low-dose conditions. The dissertation addresses bowtie-filter artifacts, cone-angle asymmetry artifacts, metal artifacts, low-dose noise, and sparse-view reconstruction through physics-aware and data-driven approaches validated with simulation and experimental studies.
